Jacob Hyatt

Game Programmer

Hello, I've been interested in games throughout my life starting from when I was younger playing sandbox games such as Zoo tycoon, Harvest Moon, or Animal Crossing. As I grew up my Uncle got me into MMORPGs such as Maplestory and FPS such as Halo and Call of duty. From there I explored the internet finding games that satsify all types of genres inbetween. My goal is to give people the feeling of getting invested into a game world as much as I was with these games




Current Games

Shatter Keep - Gameplay Programmer

SEPTEMBER 2020 - MAY 2021

Shatter Keep is an asymmetrical multiplayer game where 3 survivor players try to escape the castle and 1 player attempts to eliminate them. Survivors must complete a list of objectives to open the castle exits while arming traps, lighting torches and avoiding the monster before the timer expires.

Worked in a team of 12 to develop an asymmetrical online multiplayer game, with inspiration from ‘Among us’ and ‘Dead by Daylight’.

Responsibilities

Planned the main gameplay systems such as movement, objectives, and traps.

Developed UML diagrams for early plans of the systems and used the ‘Photon Engine’ to sync systems across the network.

Programmed and debugged these systems

Achievements

Released the game through Steam on May 7, 2021

Fully developed through remote work

Tiny Terrors - Gameplay Programmer

SEPTEMBER 2019 - MAY 2020

Worked in a team of 8 to develop a local multiplayer arcade game focused on toddlers dodging and throwing items at each other to gain points.

Responsibilities

Programmed main gameplay systems such as throwing objects, breaking objects, and point system

Testing and debugging

Mothers Garden - Lead Programmer

DECEMBER 2018 - MAY 2019

Worked in a team of 4 to develop a 2D sidescroller platformer which features small puzzles to solve with multiple interacting mechanics.

Responsibilities

Planned and programmed gameplay systems such as Follower system, Abilities, and interactable mechanics

Helped develop level and puzzle design

Challenges
